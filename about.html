About Helionics

Helionics is a federally incorporated non-profit under the Canada Not-for-profit Corporations Act (2025). Founded in 2025, Helionics is currently in its initial phase of strategic development, prototyping, and institutional engagement.

We are committed to advancing foundational and applied science at the intersection of quantum materials, energy innovation, and computational modeling. Our work integrates machine learning with quantum mechanical simulations to identify quantum-driven phenomena that surpass the limits of classical experimentation and current material synthesis techniques.

Our current research efforts are focused on the development of AI-enhanced simulation frameworks and experimental strategies to explore condensed matter systems — particularly those involving the quantum manipulation of protons and electrons. These investigations target exotic material phases such as metallic hydrogen and metastable hydrogen-based compounds.

Helionics builds upon theoretical foundations from astrophysics, solid-state physics, and thermodynamics. The platforms we are developing aim to support breakthroughs in:

  • Clean energy materials
  • Advanced electronic and magnetic states
  • Climate-relevant radiative emission modeling

Alignment with National Research Priorities

Our mission aligns directly with Canada’s strategic initiatives, including:

  • The National Quantum Strategy, fostering leadership in quantum sensing, communications, and simulation
  • The Artificial Intelligence for Design Challenge Program (NRC), supporting AI-enabled scientific discovery
  • The Critical Battery Materials Initiative, promoting next-generation materials and sustainable processing

Through ongoing development of theoretical models, software infrastructure, and experimental validation, Helionics is preparing to transition into prototyping and applied deployment phases.

We invite collaboration from academic institutions, government agencies, and industry innovators interested in pioneering new frontiers in quantum science and materials design.
